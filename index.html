REVERSING DEFORESTATION IS COMPLICATED
PLANTING TREES IS SIMPLE
WHAT'S EVEN EASIER IS JUST LETTING OUR TREES BE.
~GO PAPERLESS~

By the year 2030, we might only have 10% of Rainforests left and it can all disappear in a hundred years.

THE TROPICS LOST 11.9 MILLION HECTARES OF TREE COVER IN 2019

Logging operations, which provide the world’s wood and paper products, also fell countless trees each year.
Loggers, some of them acting illegally, also build roads to access more and more remote forests—which leads to further deforestation.

Eighty percent of Earth’s land animals and plants live in forests, and deforestation threatens species including the orangutan, Sumatran tiger, and many species of birds.

UP TO 28,000 SPECIES ARE EXPECTED TO BECOME EXTINCT BY THE NEXT QUARTER OF THE CENTURY DUE TO DEFORESTATION.

From our newspapers to our paper wrappings, paper is still everywhere and most of them are ending up in our landfills creating a staggering amount of paper waste.

42% OF ALL GLOBAL WOOD HARVEST IS USED TO MAKE PAPER.

On an average,every person uses seven trees a year in paper, wood, and other products made from trees.

We can greatly reduce our carbon footprint by simply going paperless. It's not only amazing for our forests, but can save us time, make our information secure and make dealing with data easier than ever before.
